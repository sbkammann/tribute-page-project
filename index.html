Ernest Shackleton

Time line of Ernest Shackelton's life

  • 15 February 1874 - Born in Kilkea, County Kildare, Ireland (then part of the United Kingdom)
  • 1890 - He starts an apprenticeship North Western Shipping Company, aboard the ship Hoghton Tower.
  • 1890-1894 - He spends the following four years at sea
  • August 1894 - He passes his examination for Second Mate and accepts a post as third officer on a tramp steamer of the Welsh Shire Line.
  • 1896 - He obtains his First Mate's ticket
  • 1898 - He is certified as a Master Mariner, qualifying him to command a British ship anywhere in the world.
  • 1898 - Shackleton joins Union-Castle Line, the regular mail and passenger carrier between Southampton and Cape Town
  • 17 February 1901 - His appointment as third officer to the expedition's ship Discovery is confirmed
  • 4 June 1901 - He is commissioned into the Royal Navy, with the rank of sub-lieutenant in the Reserves.
  • 31 July 1901 - He departs London on the Discovery
  • 8 January 1902 - He arrives at the Antarctic coast, via Cape Town and New Zealand.
  • 4 February - Shackleton takes part in an experimental balloon flight.
    He participates in the first sledging trip from the expedition's winter quarters in McMurdo Sound, a journey which established a safe route on to the Great Ice Barrier.
  • Winter of 1902 - Shackleton edits the expedition's magazine The South Polar Times.
  • 2 November 1902 - He reaches a record Farthest South latitude of 82° 17' with his party.
  • 11 January 1904 - He is offered, and accepts, the secretaryship of the Royal Scottish Geographical Society.
  • 9 April 1904 - He marries Emily Dorman, with whom he has three children: Raymond, Cecily, and Edward.
  • 1905 - Shackleton becomes a shareholder in a speculative company that aimes to make a fortune transporting Russian troops home from the Far East, but it fails.
  • 1906 - He ventures into politics, unsuccessfully standing in the General Election as the Liberal Unionist Party's candidate for Dundee in opposition to Irish Home Rule.
  • February 1907 - Shackleton presents to the Royal Geographical Society his plans for an Antarctic expedition. The aim is the conquest of both the geographical South Pole and the South Magnetic Pole.
  • 4 August 1907 - Shackleton is appointed a Member of the Royal Victorian Order, 4th Class.
  • 1 January 1908 - Nimrod sails for the Antarctic from Lyttelton Harbour, New Zealand.
  • 21 January 1908 - The ship arrives at the eastern sector of the Great Ice Barrier.
  • 29 October 1908 - The "Great Southern Journey", as Frank Wild called it, begins.
  • 9 January 1909 - Shackleton and three companions reach a new Farthest South latitude of 88° 23' S
  • 1910 - Shackleton makes a series of three recordings describing the expedition using an Edison Phonograph.
  • 10 July 1909 - Upon returning home King Edward VII receives him and raises him to a Commander of the Royal Victorian Order
  • November 1909 - He is made a knight, becoming Sir Ernest Shackleton
  • 3 August 1914 - Endurance is directed by the First Lord of the Admiralty, Winston Churchill, to "proceed" depsite WWI
  • 8 August 1914 - Endurance leaves British waters.
  • 27 September 1914 - Shackleton meets up with the ship in Buenos Aires.
  • 19 January 1915 - Endurance becomes frozen fast in an ice floe.
  • 24 February - Shackleton orderes the abandonment of ship's routine
  • 21 November 1915 - The wreck slips beneath the surface.
  • 24 April 1916 - The James Caird, the strongest of the lifeobats, was launches to isk an open-boat journey to the 720-nautical-mile-distant South Georgia whaling stations, where he knew help was available
  • 20 May 1916He reaches the whaling station at Stromnes
  • 30 August 1916 - With the Chilean seagoing tug Shackelton rescues the men he left behind.
  • May 1917 - He returns home and volunteers for the army
  • October 1917 - He was sent to Buenos Aires to boost British propaganda in South America, but he is unsuccessful in persuading Argentina and Chile to enter the war on the Allied side.
  • April 1918 - He returns home.
  • From October 1918 - He serves with the North Russia Expeditionary Force in the Russian Civil War under the command of Major-General Ironside, with the role of advising on the equipment and training of British forces in arctic conditions.
  • 1919 - For his services rendered in North Russia Shackleton is appointed an Officer of the Order of the British Empire
  • October 1919 - He is discharged from the army, retaining his rank of major.
  • December 1919 - Shackleton returns to the lecture circuit and publishes his own account of the Endurance expedition
  • 24 September 1921 - He leaves England on another Antarctic expedition to circumnavigate the Antarctic continent and investigate the sub-Antarctic islands
  • 5 January 1922 - Shackleton suffers a fatal heart attack
